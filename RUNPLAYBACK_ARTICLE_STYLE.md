# RunPlayBack Article Style Guide

## Purpose

Convert YouTube video transcripts into SEO-friendly written articles that feel like a professionally written review.

The goal is NOT to create a transcript summary.

The goal is to create an article similar to Engadget, Electrek, or The Verge while preserving the authentic RunPlayBack voice.

---

## Writing Style

### DO

* Write like a human rider sharing real-world experience.
* Write in first person from the RunPlayBack host's point of view, as if "I" personally installed, tested, rode, and reviewed the product.
* Use "I" for personal observations and "we" only when talking about RunPlayBack as a channel/community.
* Sound conversational and approachable.
* Use first-person observations throughout when supported by the video.
* Explain how features actually affect the riding experience.
* Prioritize ride impressions over specifications.
* Use short paragraphs.
* Use subheadings frequently.
* Focus on what matters to real riders.
* Keep typography clean and consistent. Do not use bold markdown for random phrase emphasis.

### DO NOT

* Do not sound like marketing copy.
* Do not sound like a product brochure.
* Do not repeat specifications without context.
* Do not write "Let's recap the specs."
* Do not create giant bullet lists.
* Do not overuse technical jargon.
* Do not invent information not stated in the video.
* Do not refer to the host as "he", "him", "the reviewer", "the host", or "RunPlayBack" when describing ride impressions, install experience, testing, opinions, or takeaways.
* Do not frame the article as a video recap. Avoid phrases like "this video", "in the video", "the video shows", "the video notes", or "from the video". The article should stand alone as a written review from my hands-on experience.
* Do not wrap phrases in **double asterisks** for emphasis.

---

## Article Structure

### 1. Title

Create a title that feels like a real review article.

Example:

"AINOHOT S20 Review: A Four-Wheel Electric Motobike Built for Comfort, Not Speed"

Avoid:

"AINOHOT S20 Review and Specs"

---

### 2. Introduction

Begin with an opinion, observation, or interesting hook.

Explain why the product is different.

Do NOT begin with specifications.

Bad:

"The AINOHOT S20 features a 48V battery..."

Good:

"Most electric bikes are chasing speed. The AINOHOT S20 takes a completely different approach."

Length:

2-4 paragraphs

---

### 3. First Impressions

Describe:

* size
* appearance
* build quality
* immediate reactions

Focus on rider experience.

Length:

3-5 paragraphs

---

### 4. Ride Experience

Discuss:

* acceleration
* comfort
* handling
* braking
* suspension
* power delivery

Explain how the product feels in actual use.

This should be the largest section of the article.

---

### 5. Features That Matter

Introduce features naturally.

Do not dump specifications.

Bad:

"The bike has a 48V battery and hydraulic brakes."

Good:

"The quad suspension does a surprisingly good job smoothing out rough pavement."

Explain why the feature matters.

---

### 6. Real World Use Cases

Discuss:

* commuting
* recreation
* cargo hauling
* off-road riding
* mobility applications

Help readers determine whether the product fits their needs.

---

### 7. What We Like

Use a short bulleted section.

Example:

## What We Like

* Comfortable suspension
* Stable platform
* Useful cargo capacity

---

### 8. Things To Consider

Use a short bulleted section.

Example:

## Things To Consider

* Limited top speed
* Heavy overall weight
* May not be street legal everywhere

---

### 9. Final Thoughts

Summarize:

* Who should buy it
* Who should skip it
* Overall impression

Avoid assigning numerical scores.

---

### 10. Video Section

Embed or link the original YouTube video.

---

### 11. Links Section

Include:

* Product links
* Affiliate links
* Related gear links

---

## SEO Guidelines

* Include product name in title.
* Include product name in introduction.
* Use natural keyword repetition.
* Use descriptive subheadings.
* Target article length:

  * Minimum 1,200 words
  * Ideal 1,500–2,500 words

---

## RunPlayBack Voice

The article should feel like:

* a rider talking to another rider
* honest
* practical
* experience-driven

The article should NOT feel like:

* AI generated
* a press release
* a spec sheet
* an Amazon listing

When in doubt, prioritize storytelling and real-world riding experience over specifications.
